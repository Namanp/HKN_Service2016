EECS Day

Description:

High school students got a glimpse into the curriculum and facilities that Berkeley EECS students encounter every day.

They were able to interact with Josh Hug, a UC Berkeley EECS professor through a talk and a Q&A session.

Groups of students were rotated between 3 activities which include: a department tour, a computer science lab, and an electrical engineering lab.

The day concluded with a student panel, allowing attendees to really get a first hand perspective of Berkeley EECS.

Schedule:

10:30 a.m. - 11:00 a.m. Sign In

11:00 a.m. - 11:40 a.m. Speaker

Opening Speaker was Josh Hug, a professor in UC Berkeley's Computer Science Department.

11:50 a.m. - 12:40 a.m. First Event (Department Tour)

*Students rotated between different events and might not have seen it in this order
Vice President Leo Colobong explains the insides of Cory Hall's labs.
High schoolers get the chance to see the 3D prints that Berkeley students have made and left to display.

12:40 p.m. - 1:30 p.m. Lunch

1:30 p.m. - 2:20 p.m. Second Event (Computer Science Lab)

High schoolers get help from HKN members and learn how to do an introduction computer science python lab.

2:30 p.m. - 3:20 p.m. Third Event (Electrical Engineering Lab)

Students used this MSP430 LaunchPad to build a single pixel camera.
HKN members help students out in the lab.

3:30 p.m. - 4:00 p.m. Closing Panel

High schoolers were able to get all their questions answered about the college experience with this panel of current Berkeley EECS students.
